Tactical Movement Rules
======================

This file defines the normative rules governing Tactical Movement in Assault.

Each rule is uniquely identified and traceable to concrete runtime classes
and functions.

TM-R01 — Movement as an Action
------------------------------

**Rule ID:** TM-R01

A unit moves by executing a *Movement Action*.

- Movement is an explicit action.
- Movement always consumes the unit's activation.

**Code References:**

- ``assault_model.actions.movement.MoveAction``
- ``assault_model.actions.action.Action``


TM-R02 — Legal Movement Generation
---------------------------------

**Rule ID:** TM-R02

A unit may only perform movement actions explicitly generated by the engine.

- Legal moves are produced by the Action Catalog.
- Illegal or unreachable moves cannot be selected.

**Code References:**

- ``assault_model.actions.action_catalog.ActionCatalog``
- ``ActionCatalog(state).actions()``


TM-R03 — No Pathfinding in Rules
--------------------------------

**Rule ID:** TM-R03

The rule system does not compute paths.

- Paths are precomputed when generating legal actions.
- Movement rules only evaluate the final destination.

**Code References:**

- ``MoveAction.path``
- ``ActionCatalog``


TM-R04 — Movement Execution
---------------------------

**Rule ID:** TM-R04

When a MoveAction is resolved:

- The unit's position is updated.
- No combat is initiated as part of movement resolution.
- No Victory Points are awarded immediately.

**Code References:**

- ``assault_model.runtime.runtime_game_state.RuntimeGameState.apply_action``
- ``assault_model.actions.resolution.resolve_action``


TM-R05 — Activation Consumption
--------------------------------

**Rule ID:** TM-R05

Executing a movement action consumes the unit's activation.

- After movement, the unit cannot act again until the next turn.
- The activation system determines the next active unit.

**Code References:**

- ``assault_model.core.activation_state.ActivationState``
- ``ActivationState.next_unit()``


TM-R06 — End of Turn Interaction
--------------------------------

**Rule ID:** TM-R06

Movement contributes to positional conditions evaluated at the end of the turn.

- Victory Conditions are evaluated only during turn resolution.
- Movement itself never applies scoring.

**Code References:**

- ``assault_model.core.vp_tracker.VictoryPointTracker.apply_turn``
- ``assault_model.core.turn.TurnState``


TM-R07 — Movement Termination
-----------------------------

**Rule ID:** TM-R07

If no legal movement action improves the unit's situation:

- The unit may execute a Status action instead.
- The unit remains stationary.

**Code References:**

- ``assault_model.actions.status.WaitAction``


TM-R08 — Separation from Heuristics
-----------------------------------

**Rule ID:** TM-R08

Movement rules do not encode intent.

- Rules define legality and execution.
- Intent (e.g. moving toward objectives) is external to the rule system.

**Code References:**

- ``assault_model.actions.movement.MoveAction``
- Higher-level decision systems (heuristics and strategy layers)


TM-R09 — Exclusive Hex Occupation
--------------------------------

**Rule ID:** TM-R09

At the conclusion of any action, a hex may be occupied by **at most one unit**.

Assault does not support stacking or shared hex occupation as a stable game
state.

Temporary co-location of units is permitted **only during the resolution of a
Close Combat action** and is immediately resolved. Once the combat is resolved,
only one unit may remain in the hex.

This rule applies uniformly to all movement-related actions.

**Code References:**

- ``assault_model.actions.action_catalog.ActionCatalog``
- ``assault_model.actions.movement.MoveAction``
- ``assault_model.runtime.runtime_game_state.RuntimeGameState.apply_action``
- ``assault_model.combat.close_combat``
