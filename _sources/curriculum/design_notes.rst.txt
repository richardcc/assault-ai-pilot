Design Notes
============

This document records the design decisions taken during the development
of the assault‑env reinforcement learning curriculum.

The objective is to make explicit *why* certain approaches were chosen
or rejected, particularly when simpler alternatives were intentionally
avoided.

---

Why No Reward Shaping
---------------------

Reward shaping was intentionally avoided beyond the minimal task
definition established in P1.

Although reward shaping can accelerate convergence, it often obscures
the origin of observed behaviors by embedding designer intent directly
into the optimization target.

Instead, the curriculum relies on:

- Structural changes (number of units, symmetry)
- Informational changes (observation space)
- Fixed learning algorithm (PPO)

This ensures that behavioral transitions can be attributed to scenario
structure rather than handcrafted incentives.

---

Why PPO Was Not Tuned
--------------------

PPO hyperparameters were kept constant across all curriculum stages.

This choice ensures:

- Direct behavioral comparability across P1–P4
- Diagnostic clarity when failures occur
- Avoidance of optimized but opaque behavior

Learning stability was favored over maximal task performance.

---

Deterministic vs Stochastic Evaluation
--------------------------------------

Throughout the curriculum, greedy and stochastic execution are treated
as complementary evaluation modes.

In later stages (P3, P4):

- Risky or aggressive actions exist in the policy distribution
- These actions are not always selected greedily

This divergence is interpreted as **strategic maturity**, not failure.

---

Interpreting Failure as Information
-----------------------------------

Certain curriculum stages are intentionally designed to fail under
controlled conditions.

For example:

- P2 exhibits permanent evasion
- This behavior is rational under incomplete information
- The failure exposes a structural limitation

Failures are preserved and documented rather than corrected directly.

---

Symmetry and Strategy
--------------------

In symmetric strategic environments:

- No single greedy policy dominates universally
- Outcomes depend on interaction and coordination
- Value estimation becomes inherently noisier

Behavioral analysis becomes more informative than scalar metrics.

---

Implicit vs Explicit Roles
--------------------------

Role differentiation is explored through **implicit emergence**, not
explicit assignment.

Minimal relational signals allow specialization *if* structure demands
it.

P4‑B demonstrates that ally‑centric information alone is insufficient
to induce persistent roles under symmetry.

---

Summary
-------

Across the curriculum:

- Changes are minimal and isolated
- Rewards and algorithms remain fixed
- Structure and information drive behavior

The curriculum prioritizes interpretability over performance.
