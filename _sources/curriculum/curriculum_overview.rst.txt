Curriculum Overview
===================

This document summarizes the staged curriculum used to train agents in
the assault‑env environment.

The curriculum is designed to introduce **exactly one new source of
complexity per stage**, while avoiding reward shaping and algorithmic
changes whenever possible. This incremental design isolates causal
factors and facilitates interpretable learning dynamics.

---

Curriculum Structure
--------------------

P1 – Single Unit Baseline
  Establishes basic tactical competence under purely local information.

P2 – Multi‑Unit VP Pressure (2 vs 1)
  Introduces redundancy without aggregate context, revealing a stable
  conservative failure mode.

P3 – Global Force Awareness
  Resolves the P2 failure through minimal informational augmentation.

P4 – Symmetric Strategic Engagement (2 vs 2)
  Introduces full symmetry and genuine strategic interaction.

P4‑B – Soft Role Allocation (Exploratory)
  Tests whether role‑like differentiation emerges from minimal
  ally‑centric observations.

---

Curriculum Status
-----------------

- P1–P4 are considered **canonical and frozen**
- P4‑B is **exploratory**, negative, and conclusive
- No reward shaping or PPO tuning was used at any stage

The curriculum is complete and serves as a controlled study of emergent
strategy and coordination under increasing structural complexity.
