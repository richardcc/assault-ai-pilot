P4 – Symmetric Strategic Engagement (2 vs 2)
============================================

This document reports the results of **P4**, the first fully symmetric
scenario in the assault‑env curriculum.

P4 marks the transition from localized tactical optimization to genuine
**strategic interaction**, where no trivial or universally safe policy
dominates.

---

Scenario Description
--------------------

P4 introduces a **2 vs 2 symmetric engagement** under identical rules,
terrain, and rewards as previous stages.

- Two friendly units vs two enemy units
- Identical unit types and strength
- No reward shaping
- No hyperparameter changes

All additional complexity arises from symmetry and interaction alone.

---

Training Configuration
----------------------

- Algorithm: PPO
- Policy: MultiInputPolicy
- Observation space: P3
- Total steps: ~1,000,000
- Deterministic environment

---

Training Dynamics
-----------------

Training proceeds through alternating phases of consolidation and
exploration.

The value function does not converge monotonically but stabilizes within
a bounded range, reflecting strategic ambiguity rather than instability.

---

Final Training Metrics
----------------------

At full convergence (~1M steps):

- Explained variance: fluctuates between ~0.3 and ~0.6
- Entropy remains non‑zero
- PPO updates remain stable (low KL, controlled clipping)

These signals indicate **functional convergence** under symmetry.

---

Behavioral Evaluation
---------------------

Greedy execution produces:

- Conservative, repeatable behavior
- No trivial aggression
- Risk‑aware positioning

Stochastic execution reveals:

- Selective assaults
- Victory point pressure
- Multiple viable strategies

Aggression exists within the policy distribution but is not dominant.

---

Interpretation
--------------

In symmetric strategic environments, no single greedy policy is
universally optimal.

P4 demonstrates:

- Risk‑balanced decision making
- Adaptability to opponent behavior
- Avoidance of degenerate evasion

This coexistence is interpreted as **strategic maturity**.

---

Curriculum Conclusion
---------------------

P4 fulfills its objectives and is **frozen** as a canonical curriculum
stage.
