P3 – Global Force Awareness
==========================

This document reports the results of **P3**, the third stage of the
assault‑env curriculum.

P3 introduces **global force awareness** as a corrective mechanism to
resolve the degenerate conservative behavior observed in P2.

---

Motivation
----------

In P2 (2 vs 1 without global information), the agent reliably converged
to a safe but ineffective policy characterized by:

- Constant movement
- Maximum distance from the enemy
- Zero assaults
- Zero victory point pressure

This behavior was stable and fully reproducible, indicating a **local
optimum induced by incomplete information**, rather than a failure of
the training process.

---

P3 Modification
---------------

P3 introduces **no reward shaping** and **no changes to the learning
algorithm**.

The only modification consists of extending the observation space with
aggregate force information:

- Total friendly strength
- Total enemy strength

This addition provides global situational context while preserving local
action selection and full policy symmetry.

---

Training Results
----------------

After approximately 200,000 training steps, the learning process
exhibits:

- Explained variance stabilizing around approximately 0.4
- Stable PPO update dynamics (low KL divergence, controlled clipping)
- Gradual entropy reduction without policy collapse

These metrics indicate that the value function incorporates the new
global information coherently and that learning remains well‑behaved.

---

Behavioral Evaluation
---------------------

Deterministic (greedy) evaluation remains conservative.

However, stochastic (non‑greedy) evaluation reveals qualitatively new
behavioral patterns:

- Reduced average distance to enemy units
- Selective assault behavior
- Intermittent pressure on victory point control
- Non‑identical trajectories across episodes

This indicates that aggressive and risky behaviors have entered the
**policy distribution**, even if they are not yet selected as dominant
outcomes under greedy execution.

---

Interpretation
--------------

P3 successfully resolves the local optimum observed in P2 by enabling
**situational risk assessment** based on aggregate force awareness.

However, P3 does not yet produce stable coordination or dominance:

- Aggression becomes available but is not consistently preferred
- Conservative greedy behavior remains rational
- No single strategy dominates across all situations

This outcome is expected, given the remaining asymmetry (2 vs 1) and the
absence of explicit multi‑unit strategic interaction.

---

Curriculum Role
---------------

P3 is considered:

- ✅ A successful corrective to the P2 failure mode
- ✅ Behaviorally meaningful and reproducible
- ✅ Informationally minimal

P3 is deliberately **frozen** after validation.

It serves as a **structural bridge** toward fully symmetric multi‑unit
settings, where risk‑taking can no longer be avoided through trivial
evasion.

Final Note
----------

Final training confirmed that P3 behavior remains stable at full PPO
convergence and that global force awareness is sufficient to escape the
P2 local optimum without reward intervention.
